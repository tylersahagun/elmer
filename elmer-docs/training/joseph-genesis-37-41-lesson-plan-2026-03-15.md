# Lesson Plan: Joseph in Genesis 37-41
## Come, Follow Me - Old Testament 2026, Week 11

Audience: 12-year-old Sunday School class  
Date: March 15, 2026  
Core lesson: "The Lord Was with Joseph"  
Primary scripture block: Genesis 37-41

---

## Teaching goal

Help the class see that even when life feels unfair, the Lord does not abandon His people. Joseph's story shows that the Lord can guide us, strengthen us in hardship, and prepare us to bless others.

## Big truths to repeat during the lesson

1. The Lord was with Joseph in unfair situations.
2. Revelation comes from God, and Joseph gave God the credit.
3. The Lord can prepare us in hard times to bless many people later.
4. If we prepare with faith, we do not need to fear.

---

## Suggested class plan (40-45 minutes)

## 1) Opening and hook - 5 minutes

Ask:

"Have you ever had something happen that felt unfair?"

Let two or three youth answer briefly. Then say:

"Joseph's story is full of unfair things. But the big message this week is not just that Joseph suffered. It is that the Lord was with Joseph all along."

Read aloud:

- [Genesis 39:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/39?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)
- [Genesis 39:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/39?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)

Tell the class to listen for that idea all lesson long.

---

## 2) Story section 1: Joseph's early life and dreams - 8 minutes

### Scriptures

- [Genesis 37:3-11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/37?lang=eng&id=p3-p11#p3)
- [Genesis 37:18-28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/37?lang=eng&id=p18-p28#p18)

### Teach simply

- Jacob loved Joseph and gave him a special coat.
- Joseph had inspired dreams.
- His brothers became jealous.
- They threw him into a pit and sold him into Egypt.

### Questions

- Why do you think Joseph's brothers were so upset?
- What do you think Joseph may have felt when he was betrayed?
- If you were Joseph, what would have been hard about this moment?

### Teaching point

Even when other people make wrong choices, the Lord still sees us and can still guide our future.

---

## 3) Story section 2: The Lord helped Joseph in hardship - 10 minutes

### Scriptures

- [Genesis 39:1-6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/39?lang=eng&id=p1-p6#p1)
- [Genesis 39:20-23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/39?lang=eng&id=p20-p23#p20)

### Teach simply

- Joseph served in Potiphar's house.
- The Lord blessed Joseph and helped him.
- Joseph was later falsely accused and sent to prison.
- Even in prison, the Lord was with him.

### Important note

Because of your class plan, keep this section centered on hardship, trust, and the Lord's help. Briefly mention that Joseph chose righteousness and then faced unfair consequences, but do not spend time on the temptation discussion.

### Personal story moment

Share your two stories here:

1. Pre-mission story  
   Focus prompt: "What hardship was I facing, and how did I see that the Lord was with me even before everything was fixed?"

2. Mission president conversation  
   Focus prompt: "What truth or counsel helped me understand that the Lord helps us through trials, not always by removing them right away?"

### Questions after your stories

- How was the Lord with Joseph?
- How has the Lord helped you in something hard?
- What is the difference between "easy life" and "God's help"?

### Teaching point

Following the Lord does not always remove hardship, but it does bring strength, direction, and peace.

---

## 4) Story section 3: Joseph interprets dreams - 8 minutes

### Scriptures

- [Genesis 40:5-8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/40?lang=eng&id=p5-p8#p5)
- [Genesis 41:14-16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p14-p16#p14)
- [Genesis 41:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p25#p25), [Genesis 41:37-39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p37-p39#p37)

### Activity

Play your prepared "guess the dreams" game here.

### Debrief after the game

Make sure the class hears these points:

- Joseph listened carefully.
- Joseph said interpretation belongs to God.
- Joseph used his gift to help others, not to glorify himself.

### Questions

- What do you like about Joseph's answer in [Genesis 41:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)?
- Why is it important that Joseph gave glory to God?
- What can we do when we need help understanding the Lord's will?

### Teaching point

Revelation is real, and humble people point back to God when they receive help from Him.

---

## 5) Story section 4: Joseph prepares for famine - 7 minutes

### Scriptures

- [Genesis 41:28-36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p28-p36#p28)
- [Genesis 41:46-49](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p46-p49#p46)
- [Genesis 41:53-57](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/41?lang=eng&id=p53-p57#p53)
- [Doctrine and Covenants 38:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/38?lang=eng&id=p30#p30)

### Teach simply

- Pharaoh dreamed about years of plenty and years of famine.
- Joseph understood the warning and suggested a wise plan.
- Food was stored during the good years.
- When the famine came, that preparation blessed many people.

### Questions

- What did Joseph do when God warned him?
- How can preparation be a spiritual act?
- What are some ways a 12-year-old can prepare now?

### Good answers you can draw out

- prayer and scripture study
- learning to hear the Spirit
- choosing good friends
- obeying parents
- preparing for future covenants and service

### Teaching point

The Lord often prepares us before the hard time comes.

---

## 6) Wrap-up and testimony - 5 minutes

Return to the main message:

"The Lord was with Joseph."

Ask the class to finish this sentence:

"One thing I learned from Joseph is ..."

Then bear short testimony using words like these:

"Joseph's life shows me that the Lord is aware of us in betrayal, loneliness, waiting, and responsibility. He can guide us in confusing times, strengthen us in painful times, and prepare us to help other people."

Invite the youth to look this week for one moment when they can say:

"The Lord was with me."

Then pause at the teaching lane:

- The Lord is with us in hardship.
- God gives revelation.
- Preparation blesses many people.

If you want one extra connection from Scripture Helps, briefly mention that Joseph points to Jesus Christ in several ways:

- both were beloved sons
- both were rejected and betrayed
- both suffered unfairly but remained faithful

Keep that part short and simple for this age group.

---

## Optional backup ideas if time changes

If class is shorter:

- shorten the opening discussion
- tell only the strongest parts of your two personal stories
- do one or two debrief questions after the dreams game instead of several

If class is longer:

- ask youth to identify every time Joseph gave credit to God
- have them list ways the Lord can be "with us" that are not always dramatic
- let them share one area where they want to prepare now for the future

---

## One-sentence takeaway

Because the Lord was with Joseph in every stage of his journey, He can also help us in unfair, confusing, and important moments of our own lives.
