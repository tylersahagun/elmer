# Joseph in Genesis 37-41

## Sunday School lesson plan

Audience: 12-year-old class  
Manual base: Come, Follow Me - For Home and Church: Old Testament 2026, March 9-15, lesson 11, "The Lord Was with Joseph"  
Primary scriptures: Genesis 37-41  
Suggested length: 35-45 minutes

## Main lesson idea

Even when Joseph's life felt unfair, the Lord was with him. The Lord can also help us in hard times, guide us through revelation, and prepare us now so we can bless other people later.

## Your custom teaching choices

- Skip the detailed temptation discussion from Genesis 39.
- Use your prepared dream-guessing game during the dream section.
- Share two personal stories during the hardship section:
  - your pre-mission story
  - your story about a conversation with your mission president

## Learning goals for the class

By the end of the lesson, the class should be able to:

1. Retell the main journey of Joseph in Genesis 37-41.
2. Notice that the Lord stayed with Joseph in every stage of the story.
3. Explain that the Lord can guide us and prepare us through hard experiences.
4. Identify one way they can trust the Lord more this week.

## Materials

- Scriptures for each student if possible
- The tldraw diagram of Joseph's journey
- Whiteboard or chalkboard
- Your prepared dream-guessing game
- Pen or small paper slips for a short closing challenge

## Short scripture map

- Genesis 37:3-11 - Joseph's coat and dreams
- Genesis 37:18-28 - Joseph is betrayed and sold
- Genesis 39:1-6 - The Lord blesses Joseph in Potiphar's house
- Genesis 39:20-23 - The Lord is with Joseph in prison
- Genesis 40:5-8 - Joseph says interpretation belongs to God
- Genesis 41:14-16 - Joseph is brought before Pharaoh
- Genesis 41:25-36 - Joseph interprets the dreams and gives wise counsel
- Genesis 41:38-43 - Joseph is chosen to lead
- Genesis 41:46-49, 53-57 - Joseph prepares for famine and blesses many people

## Lesson flow

### 1) Opening and connection question - 3 to 5 minutes

Ask:

"Have you ever had something unfair happen even when you were trying to do what was right?"

Let a few students answer. Then say:

"Today we are going to follow Joseph's journey from a favorite son, to a slave, to a prisoner, to a ruler in Egypt. The biggest truth we will watch for is this: the Lord was with Joseph."

Show the tldraw diagram and point out that the board will help the class follow the whole journey.

### 2) Walk through Joseph's journey - 10 to 12 minutes

Use the diagram as your visual guide and move left to right through the story.

#### Part A - Dreams and jealousy

Read or summarize Genesis 37:3-11.

Teaching point:

Joseph was loved by his father and received dreams that showed the Lord had a future purpose for him, but his brothers became jealous.

Ask:

- Why do you think Joseph's brothers reacted so strongly?
- Have you ever seen jealousy make a problem worse?

#### Part B - Betrayal and Egypt

Read or summarize Genesis 37:18-28.

Teaching point:

Joseph was thrown into a pit and sold. Even when other people made cruel choices, the Lord still had not forgotten Joseph.

Ask:

- What might Joseph have felt at this point?
- Why is it important to remember that a hard moment is not the end of the story?

#### Part C - Potiphar's house and prison

Summarize Genesis 39:1-6 and 39:20-23.

Keep this section brief, since you want to skip the detailed temptation discussion. You can simply say:

"Joseph kept choosing what was right, and later he was falsely accused and sent to prison. But the scriptures still say that the Lord was with him."

Ask students to look for the repeated truth:

- Genesis 39:2
- Genesis 39:3
- Genesis 39:21
- Genesis 39:23

Ask:

"What do these verses teach us about God when life is hard?"

### 3) Dream game and revelation discussion - 8 to 10 minutes

Bridge into the game by reading Genesis 40:8 and Genesis 41:16.

Key teaching line:

"Joseph did not act like the power came from him. He pointed to God."

Run your prepared dream-guessing game here.

After the game, debrief with questions like:

- Why do you think dreams got so much attention in Joseph's story?
- What did Joseph understand about where true help came from?
- What is the difference between being clever and being guided by God?

Simple doctrine statement:

The Lord can help us understand what we need to know, especially when we humbly turn to Him.

### 4) Personal stories: the Lord helps in hardship - 8 to 10 minutes

This is the heart of your lesson.

Transition line:

"Joseph's story is powerful because it shows that faith in God does not always make life easier right away, but it does mean we are never alone."

#### Story 1 - Your pre-mission story

Share your story in a simple pattern:

1. What hardship or uncertainty you were facing
2. What you did to seek the Lord
3. How the Lord helped, strengthened, or guided you
4. What you learned

#### Story 2 - Conversation with your mission president

Share:

1. What challenge or question was on your mind
2. What your mission president said
3. How that counsel showed the Lord's awareness of you
4. How it connects to Joseph's life

After both stories, ask:

- How can the Lord be with us even if the problem does not disappear immediately?
- When have you felt helped, strengthened, warned, or comforted by the Lord?

### 5) Joseph prepares and blesses others - 5 to 7 minutes

Read or summarize Genesis 41:25-36 and 41:46-49, 53-57.

Teaching point:

Joseph's earlier trials helped prepare him for a moment when he could save many lives. The Lord was not only blessing Joseph. He was preparing Joseph to bless others.

Ask:

- How did Joseph use wisdom and preparation after receiving revelation?
- What are some ways we can prepare now so we can help others later?

Possible answers to draw out:

- learning the gospel
- listening to promptings
- building trust with God
- practicing obedience in small things
- preparing spiritually and temporally

### 6) Closing testimony and challenge - 3 minutes

Close with this summary:

- The Lord was with Joseph in the pit.
- The Lord was with Joseph in slavery.
- The Lord was with Joseph in prison.
- The Lord was with Joseph when he stood before Pharaoh.
- The Lord can be with us too.

Invite each class member to finish this sentence on paper or aloud:

"This week I will trust the Lord by ..."

Bear brief testimony that the Lord knows them personally and can help them in unfair, confusing, or delayed situations.

## Questions to keep handy during the lesson

- Where do you see the Lord in this part of Joseph's story?
- What changed in Joseph's life, and what stayed true the whole time?
- What did Joseph do when he did not control his circumstances?
- How can the Lord help us understand what to do next?
- How can hard things prepare us to bless someone else?

## Suggested one-sentence take-home message

The Lord was with Joseph, and He can be with me in hard things too.

## Optional handout or board phrase

Pit to palace, but the real miracle was this: the Lord was with Joseph the whole way.

## Notes on alignment with the official lesson

This lesson stays centered on the current Come, Follow Me theme "The Lord Was with Joseph" and the scripture block Genesis 37-41. It intentionally de-emphasizes the detailed temptation discussion from Genesis 39 in order to spend more class time on:

- the full story arc
- revelation through dreams
- the Lord's help in hardship
- preparation that blesses others

Those choices fit well for a 12-year-old class and still remain true to the main doctrine of the lesson.
