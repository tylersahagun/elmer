## Gong (2025-2026 Updates)

**AI Meeting Prep Page** (GA)

- Pre-meeting summary with objectives, key topics, prior discussion highlights, and action items

- "Ask Anything" — query the full account/deal context (use cases, challenges, goals)

Generates structured account/deal briefs unifying conversations, emails, web intelligence, and CRM data. Customizable brief types for meeting prep, deal review, and account handoffs.

## Cross-Competitor Pattern Analysis

### Emerging Trends (2025-2026)

1. **Meeting prep is the new battleground.** Gong, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Sybill, PrepMe (Tektonic AI), Chili Piper, and Pod all launched or expanded meeting prep features in 2025. The market recognizes that reps spend only ~30% of their week selling — prep automation is a clear value lever.

2. **"Ask anything" is table stakes.** Gong (Ask Anything), Fathom (Ask Fathom), Fireflies (AskFred), Avoma (Ask Avoma) — every player now offers a conversational AI interface over meeting data. AskElephant must have this, but it's not a differentiator.

3. **Trust and transparency are emerging differentiators.** HubSpot's audit cards, Salesforce's Agent Script for predictable behavior, and Gong's AI Trainer all reflect growing market demand for explainable AI. AskElephant's approval/evidence model aligns with this trend.

4. **Credit/consumption pricing is spreading.** Salesforce ($2/conversation), HubSpot (credit-based), Fireflies (AI credit add-on) — the market is moving toward usage-based AI pricing. AskElephant should consider how the Chief of Staff features map to value-based pricing.

5. **Mini apps / modular AI are a distribution play.** Fireflies' mini apps and Salesforce's AgentExchange show a trend toward composable AI features. AskElephant's sub-initiative structure (summary, prep, brief, actions) naturally maps to this pattern.

6. **Post-acquisition stagnation is real.** Chorus (ZoomInfo, 2021), Groove (Clari, 2023), Wingman/Copilot (Clari, 2022) — acquired products consistently show slower innovation. This creates openings for focused players.

### Strategic Implications

1. **Daily Brief and Weekly Brief are the strongest moats.** No competitor is even attempting these. Ship them first.
2. **Action queue differentiation is defensible** but must be paired with CRM integration depth to prevent "nice but disconnected" perception.
3. **Meeting prep is contested territory.** Gong's prep is already strong. AskElephant must win on persona-awareness and cross-signal enrichment, not just matching Gong's account-centric prep.
4. **Meeting summary is crowded.** Fathom's 5.0/5 G2 rating and free tier set a very high UX bar. The editable artifact + AI edit model must demonstrably outperform view-only summaries.
5. **Trust/transparency is a rising tide.** HubSpot audit cards and Salesforce Agent Script show the market moving toward AskElephant's values. Lean into this as positioning, not just features.
