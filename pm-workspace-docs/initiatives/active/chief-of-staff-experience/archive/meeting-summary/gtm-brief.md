## Value Proposition

### For Sales Reps

> **Before:** You finish a customer call and have to navigate workflow settings to get a summary, then rewrite it manually because the format doesn't match what you need. By the time you've got something shareable, 15 minutes have passed and you've already lost momentum on follow-ups.

> **After:** Open your meeting — the summary is already there, formatted for the type of call you just had. Edit one section with AI in 10 seconds. Check evidence links to verify a key quote. Share with confidence in under 90 seconds. Your follow-ups are already structured and ready to execute.

**One-liner:** Meeting summaries that match how you work — choose your format, edit with AI, verify with evidence, share with confidence.

### For Sales Leaders

> **Before:** Reviewing team calls means reading inconsistent summaries or listening to full recordings. Each rep formats their notes differently. Spotting coaching moments or deal risks across 20 meetings a week is a manual slog.

> **After:** Your team's meetings use standardized templates — QBRs look like QBRs, demos look like demos. You scan structured sections for risk signals and coaching moments across your team in minutes, not hours. You trust the highlights because every insight links back to the source.

**One-liner:** Standardized, evidence-backed meeting summaries across your team — spot coaching moments and deal risks at a glance.

### For Customer Success Managers

> **Before:** Preparing for renewal conversations means piecing together notes from different QBRs, each formatted differently. You can't quickly see sentiment trends or share relevant highlights with the account team without manual work.

> **After:** All your QBRs use the same template with structured sections for metrics review, risks, and expansion signals. Evidence-linked sentiment indicators give you confidence in escalation decisions. Share curated sections with internal stakeholders in one click.

**One-liner:** Consistent QBR summaries with evidence-backed insights — prepare for renewals and align your team in minutes.

## Positioning vs. Competitors

### Primary Competitive Narrative

**Category:** Meeting Intelligence / Conversation Intelligence

**Positioning statement:** AskElephant Meeting Summary is the first **editable, templatized meeting artifact** in conversation intelligence. While competitors deliver fixed-format post-meeting briefs you can only read, AskElephant lets you choose your format, edit sections with AI, save your preferences, and verify every insight against the source — turning meeting summaries from disposable AI output into trusted, reusable business artifacts.

### Head-to-Head Positioning

| Competitor    | Their Strength                                | Our Differentiation                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fathom**    | Free tier, extreme ease of use, 5.0 G2 rating | Fathom summaries are read-only. AskElephant summaries are editable artifacts with template selection, AI edit, and save-as-template. You don't just read — you shape.                  |
| **Gong**      | Established brand, deep analytics, AI Briefer | Gong's summary is a fixed-format brief. AskElephant lets you pick the format by meeting type and edit sections individually. Evidence links show exactly where each insight came from. |
| **Fireflies** | Multi-platform recording, mini-app formats    | Fireflies offers pre-built formats but no in-context template switching or section-level AI editing. AskElephant lets you rewrite a section without regenerating the whole summary.    |
| **Clari**     | Revenue intelligence platform integration     | Clari's summaries are analytics inputs, not user-facing artifacts. AskElephant treats the summary as the primary meeting output — front and center, not buried in analytics.           |

### Competitive Landmines (Objection Handling)

**"Fathom is free and 5-star rated, why should I pay for this?"**
→ Fathom is excellent for individual note-taking. AskElephant's summary is a team-level business artifact — templated by meeting type, editable, evidence-backed, and privacy-controlled for sharing. When your team needs consistent meeting outputs that drive execution, not just personal notes, that's what we built.

**"Gong already gives us meeting summaries."**
→ Gong gives you a fixed-format brief. If it doesn't match what your team needs — say, for a discovery call vs. a QBR — you're stuck. AskElephant lets you pick the template, edit any section with AI, and save your format as the default. One meeting summary system that adapts to how you actually work.

**"We don't need another summary tool."**
→ This isn't another tool — it's built into your existing meeting view. No new login, no new app. Open a meeting, and the summary is already there in the right format. It's about making what you already have more useful.

## Feedback Collection Plan

### In-App Micro-Feedback

- **Trigger:** After 5th summary view
- **Format:** Single question: "How useful are your meeting summaries?" (1-5 star scale)
- **Follow-up (if ≤3 stars):** Optional text: "What would make them more useful?"
- **Frequency cap:** Once per user per 30 days

### Beta Feedback Loop

- **Beta check-in:** Scheduled 15-minute call with 5 beta accounts at Beta + 2 weeks
- **Focus areas:** Template usefulness, AI edit quality, evidence link trust, share workflow friction
- **Capture tool:** Transcript → `/research meeting-summary` for analysis

### Analytics Monitoring (PostHog)

- **Dashboard:** Meeting Summary feature dashboard with all success metrics (see PRD)
- **Alerts:** Set up alerts for guardrail metric thresholds (quality complaints >10/week, edit rejection >40%)
- **Cohort:** Track beta cohort vs. GA cohort adoption curves

### Quarterly Review

- **Template usage analysis:** Which templates are most/least used? Signal for template improvements
- **AI edit patterns:** What instructions do users give most? Signal for default behavior improvements
- **Evidence link engagement:** Click-through rate by section type → Signal for extraction quality priorities
