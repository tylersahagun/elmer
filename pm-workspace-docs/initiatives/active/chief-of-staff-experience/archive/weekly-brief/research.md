# Research: Weekly Brief

## Summary

Weekly Brief is the accountability layer for Chief of Staff, intended to preserve continuity and reduce forgotten commitments between weeks. Users lack one reliable view of trends, completed work, unresolved risk, and next commitments. Manual synthesis is inconsistent.

---

## Jobs to Be Done

**When** I run my weekly planning or review cycle,  
**I want to** see what changed this week and what must carry forward,  
**So that** I can reset execution, reduce missed follow-through, and improve forecast confidence.

---

## Evidence Quotes

> "Users need a clear carry-forward mechanism, not just static recap." — Internal synthesis
> "Weekly planning is often disconnected from daily actions and post-meeting commitments." — Internal synthesis
> "Historical visibility is necessary for trust in trend narratives." — Internal synthesis

_Note: External customer quotes to be added during interview-plan feedback._

---

## Key Findings

1. **Carry-forward is essential** — Static weekly recap is insufficient. Users need explicit "what didn't get done and moves to next week."
2. **Disconnect between daily and weekly** — Daily actions and post-meeting commitments often don't flow into weekly planning.
3. **Historical visibility builds trust** — Trend narratives require "what did we say last week?" for consistency.
4. **Decision support** — Weekly brief should support decisions: commit, defer, drop.

## Open Questions

1. **Trend windows** — Which trend windows (7d, 14d, MTD) are required for v1?
2. **Lock vs edit** — Should weekly brief lock after publishing or allow edits?
3. **Carry-forward rules** — Automatic vs user-tagged? Priority-based?
4. **Persona-specific sections** — Same brief for reps and leaders or different emphasis?
