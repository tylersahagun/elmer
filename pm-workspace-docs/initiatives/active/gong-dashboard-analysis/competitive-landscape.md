# Competitive Landscape: Dashboard & Data Visualization in Meeting Intelligence

> **Last Analyzed:** 2026-03-01
> **Competitors Evaluated:** 7 (3 Direct, 2 Indirect, 2 Adjacent)
> **Analysis Trigger:** Recurring competitive losses to Gong on dashboard/analytics visibility
> **Differentiation Score:** Strong opportunity — AskElephant's automation data is unique in the market

---

## TL;DR

The meeting intelligence dashboard landscape is dominated by Gong's comprehensive analytics suite, with Avoma emerging as a mid-market challenger and Clari/Salesloft converging on revenue intelligence. Every serious competitor offers some form of team analytics and coaching metrics. AskElephant is the **only platform with zero user-facing dashboards** despite having uniquely rich automation data (CRM updates, workflow execution, cross-system actions). The competitive matrix shows three zones: table stakes we must match (conversation metrics, team views), parity features that matter (coaching scorecards, pipeline views), and leapfrog opportunities unique to AskElephant (automation ROI, cross-system intelligence, zero-config analytics).

## Competitor Profiles

### Gong (Direct — Category Leader)

**Dashboard Offering:** Most comprehensive in the market with 6 pre-built dashboard types (Pipeline Analysis, Competitive Analysis, Scorecards Analysis, Economic Pulse, Forecast Analytics, Team Insights) plus a custom dashboard builder with widgets, charts, and KPIs.

**Key Differentiators:**
- Custom dashboard builder with drag-and-drop widgets
- Talk-to-listen ratio backed by proprietary research (43:57 optimal)
- Smart Trackers for topic/competitor detection
- Deal Board with AI risk flagging
- Configurable Forecast Boards

**Pricing:** $100-200/user/month + $5K-75K platform fee. Full stack with Clari reaches $410-550/user/month.

**Dashboard Weaknesses:**
- Forecast rated 4/10 by users (40% also buy Clari)
- 3-6 month implementation for full analytics
- Smart Trackers require 50-100 training examples, 40+ hrs/mo maintenance
- No automation outcome metrics — can only show what happened in calls, not what agents did after
- No product usage metrics for client health

**Market Position:** Category leader, 5,000+ customers, G2 4.8/5 (6,200+ reviews). Gartner MQ Leader for Revenue Action Orchestration.

---

### Chorus/ZoomInfo (Direct — Enterprise)

**Dashboard Offering:** Conversation analytics with ZoomInfo enrichment. Call pattern tracking, competitive mentions, deal intelligence views.

**Key Differentiators:**
- ZoomInfo integration for contact/company enrichment
- Enterprise-grade security and compliance
- Deep Salesforce integration

**Dashboard Weaknesses:**
- Less custom dashboard flexibility than Gong
- Product direction less clear post-acquisition
- Limited mid-market appeal ($800-1,200/user/year)

**Market Position:** Enterprise-focused, declining mindshare vs. Gong.

---

### Avoma (Direct — Mid-Market Challenger)

**Dashboard Offering:** Activity Dashboard with org-wide stats, Conversation Insights with interaction analytics, Revenue Insights with CRM mapping, AI Coaching Agent with custom scorecards.

**Key Differentiators:**
- AI Coaching Agent automates 100% call scoring (not just reviewed calls)
- Custom scorecard templates for methodology-specific coaching
- Price-accessible ($19/user/month)
- Full-stack: recording → analytics → coaching in one tool

**Dashboard Weaknesses:**
- No custom dashboard builder
- Smaller market presence than Gong
- Less sophisticated pipeline/forecast views
- No automation outcome metrics

**Market Position:** Fastest-growing mid-market option. Strong on coaching automation.

---

### Fireflies.ai (Indirect — Integration-First)

**Dashboard Offering:** Basic conversation analytics (sentiment, objections, competitors). CRM integration for deal context. Team meeting analytics.

**Key Differentiators:**
- 69-language support (best in market)
- Deep CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive)
- Affordable ($10-19/user/month)
- Broad integration ecosystem

**Dashboard Weaknesses:**
- No custom dashboard builder
- Limited pipeline/forecast views
- Analytics less sophisticated than Gong/Avoma
- More meeting-capture-first than analytics-first

**Market Position:** Volume player at mid-market. Wins on price and integrations.

---

### Fathom (Indirect — Individual Productivity)

**Dashboard Offering:** Minimal — individual meeting history, searchable archive, no team analytics.

**Key Differentiators:**
- Highest transcription accuracy (~95%)
- Unlimited free recordings
- Simplest user experience in market

**Dashboard Weaknesses:**
- No team analytics whatsoever
- No coaching, pipeline, or custom dashboards
- Not designed for revenue teams

**Market Position:** Dominant at individual/solo use. Not competitive for team analytics.

---

### Clari/Salesloft (Adjacent — Revenue Intelligence)

**Dashboard Offering:** Best-in-class forecasting with AI prediction, deal inspection agents, NRR tracking, trend analysis, churn risk detection.

**Key Differentiators:**
- Revenue forecasting accuracy (the reason 40% of Gong customers also buy Clari)
- AI agents for deal analysis and pipeline inspection
- Time Series Data Hub for historical patterns
- 75,000+ revenue teams

**Dashboard Weaknesses:**
- No meeting recording or conversation analytics
- Enterprise-first (complex, expensive)
- Not a daily workflow tool for reps
- Post-merger product consolidation uncertainty

**Market Position:** Forecast leader. Complementary to (not replacement for) meeting intelligence tools.

## UX Pattern Inventory

### Pattern 1: Team Performance Dashboard

| Competitor | Approach | Strength | Weakness |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Gong** | Team Insights tab with stats updated 2x/day. Talk ratio, questions, interactivity, monologue per rep. Filter by team/date/call type. | Comprehensive, research-backed benchmarks, zero-effort data | Complex UI, information dense, requires training |
| **Avoma** | Activity Dashboard with org-wide stats, filterable by team/user/purpose/stage | Clean filtering, includes internal/external split | Less metric variety than Gong |
| **Chorus** | Team analytics with ZoomInfo enrichment | Enterprise-grade data quality | Less accessible UX |

**Emerging best practice:** Card-based rep profiles with sparkline trends + drill-down to individual detail. Filter by time range and call type. Benchmark against team average.

### Pattern 2: Self-Coaching / Individual Analytics

| Competitor | Approach | Strength | Weakness |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Gong** | Personal stats in Team Insights, comparison to team average | Objective data for self-improvement | Buried in team view, not dedicated surface |
| **Avoma** | AI Coaching Agent scores every call automatically | 100% coverage, no manager dependency | Less self-service exploration |
| **Fathom** | Meeting history with AI summaries | Simple, fast, personal | No analytics or trends |

**Emerging best practice:** Dedicated "My Performance" view with trend lines over time. AI coaching suggestions based on patterns. Comparison to team average (anonymous). Progress tracking toward personal goals.

### Pattern 3: Pipeline Intelligence

| Competitor | Approach | Strength | Weakness |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Gong** | Deal Board with AI risk flags, CRM field configuration, multiple boards | Most comprehensive pipeline + conversation intelligence | Requires CRM setup, expensive add-on |
| **Clari** | AI-predicted deal outcomes, forecast accuracy tracking | Best forecasting accuracy | No conversation intelligence |
| **Avoma** | Revenue Insights with CRM field mapping, win/loss analysis | Good mid-market option | Less sophisticated than Gong/Clari |

**Emerging best practice:** Pipeline view enriched with conversation signals (last touch, risk signals, competitive mentions). Don't try to replace CRM — augment it with intelligence the CRM doesn't have.

### Pattern 4: Automation Outcome Dashboard (AskElephant Unique — No Market Reference)

**No competitor has this pattern.** We're creating a new category: "automation intelligence dashboards."

**Proposed pattern:**
- Hero KPIs: Total workflows run, CRM records updated, deals influenced, time saved estimate
- Breakdown by automation type: CRM updates, email processing, Slack actions, meeting processing
- Trend lines showing automation volume and quality over time
- Success/failure rates with transparency into what went wrong
- Per-user automation impact: "AskElephant saved you 12 hours this month"
- Per-deal automation attribution: "These 3 deals had AskElephant-generated CRM updates"

## Strategic Risks If We Don't Act

1. **Continued competitive losses to Gong** — Dashboard visibility is a key evaluation criterion. Every deal that involves team analytics comparison, we lose.

2. **"Invisible automation" problem compounds** — As we build more agents and workflows, the gap between what we DO and what users can SEE widens. This is a trust problem, not just a feature gap.

3. **RevOps lock-in at Gong** — Once RevOps builds custom dashboards in Gong, switching costs skyrocket. Every month we wait, their investment in Gong's analytics deepens.

4. **Coaching differentiation closes** — Avoma's AI Coaching Agent is automating what Gong requires manual effort for. If we don't enter the coaching analytics space, both competitors will own it.

5. **Market expectation is shifting** — The meeting intelligence market ($6.5B by 2026) now expects analytics as table stakes. Offering a product without user-facing dashboards is increasingly indefensible.
