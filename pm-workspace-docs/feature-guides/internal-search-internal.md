# Internal Search: Quick Answers for Revenue

**Purpose:** Help reps, leaders, and CSMs find conversation answers fast.

---

## What It Is (Simple)

Internal Search is "Google for your customer conversations." Ask a question in chat and it finds answers from
meetings, transcripts, summaries, and notes your team already has.

---

## What it searches

- Meeting recordings, transcripts, summaries, and notes available to the user.
- Results always respect workspace permissions.

---

## When To Use It

- **Prep** before a call
- **Recall** past decisions or commitments
- **Coach** by scanning patterns across meetings
- **Renew** with full account context

---

## How To Turn It On

1. Open **Global Chat** or any chat in AskElephant.
2. Click **Tools** (puzzle icon).
3. Toggle **Internal search** ON.

If you do not see the toggle, the workspace does not have it enabled yet.

---

## How To Ask (Best Results)

- Use plain English.
- Include a **time range** ("last 30 days", "this quarter").
- Name a **company or contact** when possible.

---

## Example Questions (Copy/Paste)

**For reps**

- "What did we discuss with Acme in the last 30 days?"
- "What objections came up on my last call with Sarah Johnson?"
- "What commitments did I make to TechStart last month?"

**For leaders**

- "Show me discovery calls where pricing came up last week."
- "Which accounts mentioned security concerns this month?"

**For CSMs**

- "What were the key risks raised by NovaCorp this quarter?"
- "What success metrics did we commit to for DataStream?"

---

## If You Don’t Find What You Need

1. Widen the time range.
2. Try a different phrase.
3. Make sure the meeting was recorded.
4. Ask Support if you still can’t find it.

---

## Limitations

- Only searches recorded meetings and stored AskElephant data.
- External data sources require the relevant integrations to be enabled.
