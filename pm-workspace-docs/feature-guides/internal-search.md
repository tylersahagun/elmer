# Internal Search: Help Center Guide

**Feature status:** Available  
**Availability:** Workspaces with Internal Search enabled

---

## What Is Internal Search?

Internal Search lets you ask questions about your customer conversations in plain English. It finds answers from
meetings, transcripts, summaries, notes, and chats—so you spend less time hunting and more time acting.

---

## Who It's For

- **Sales reps**: prep for calls, recall commitments, find objections
- **Sales leaders**: coaching and pipeline visibility at scale
- **CSMs**: renewal prep, escalation context, expansion signals
- **RevOps**: audit capture coverage, validate process adherence

---

## Where to Find It

1. Open **Global Chat** or any chat in AskElephant.
2. Click **Tools** (puzzle icon) in the chat composer.
3. Toggle **Internal search** (purple database icon).

If you do not see the toggle, your workspace may not have Internal Search enabled yet.

---

## How It Works

1. Ask a question in natural language.
2. Internal Search looks across your meeting history and conversations.
3. Results appear in an **Internal search results** panel.
4. Ask a follow‑up question to narrow or refine the results.

---

## What it searches

- Meeting recordings, transcripts, summaries, notes, and chats available in AskElephant.
- Only data you have permission to view.
- External systems (CRM, docs, ticketing) require the relevant integrations to be enabled.

---

## Example Questions

**Sales reps**

- "What did we discuss with Acme in the last 30 days?"
- "Show me objections from my last call with Sarah Johnson."

**Sales leaders**

- "Find discovery calls where pricing came up last week."
- "Show me meetings with enterprise accounts this month."

**CSMs**

- "What risks did DataStream mention this quarter?"
- "Show me all meetings tied to NovaCorp before renewal."

**RevOps**

- "Which meetings from this week do not have summaries?"
- "Who has not recorded a meeting in the last 14 days?"

---

## Tips for Better Results

- **Add time windows:** "last 2 weeks", "in December", "Q4"
- **Use natural language:** Internal Search understands meaning, not just keywords
- **Start broad, then narrow:** get a shortlist first, then drill into one account

---

## Common Questions & Answers

**Does it search transcripts or summaries?**  
Both. It can search transcripts and summaries.

**How far back does it search?**  
As far back as the workspace data exists.

**Can I search meetings I wasn't on?**  
Yes, as long as you have permission to view them.

**Does it pull CRM data?**  
Not directly. CRM data requires the relevant integrations to be enabled.

---

## Troubleshooting

**"Internal search" tool is missing**

- The feature is not enabled for your workspace.
- Ask your admin to enable Internal Search.

**No results found**

- Try a broader time window.
- Confirm the meeting was recorded.
- Ask using a different phrase (semantic search helps but not all terms match).

**Results look incomplete**

- Try a narrower question that targets a single company or contact.

---

## Known Limitations

- **Availability**: not visible if Internal Search is not enabled in your workspace.
- **Data scope**: only searches stored AskElephant data (meetings, chats, signals).
- **Permissions**: results respect workspace access controls.
- **No unrecorded meetings**: meetings without recordings/metadata will not appear.

---

## Need Help?

If Internal Search is not showing up or results look off, reach out to your AskElephant contact or support.
