## TL;DR

Rob reviewed the v9 Agent Command Center prototype and expressed strong excitement about the vision ("I would pay lots of money for that right now") but identified a critical gap: **the experience is insight-driven when it should be action-driven**. His core feedback is that AskElephant should think ahead 5 steps and present what he _should do_, not just show him data and let him figure it out. He introduced a powerful framing: **"When I'm proactive, AI is reactive. When AI is proactive, I'm reactive."** This implies two distinct modes — a planning mode where the user leads and a rapid-fire mode where AskElephant leads. He also emphasized that the experience should be **dynamic based on time of day**, learning the user's system over time, and introducing new features contextually rather than through announcements.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Action-Driven > Insight-Driven (Critical)

Rob's strongest and most repeated feedback: the prototype shows information but doesn't tell him what to **do**. He wants AskElephant to think through the next 5 steps and present actionable recommendations, not dashboards.

> "To me, conversational AI, I'm stoked about because it should help me narrow my focus with whatever is most important right now, and then work with me on that."
> — Rob Henderson [0:12:44]

> "If you focus on the action, then the insights are a byproduct of what should be done."
> — Rob Henderson [0:17:01]

> "It's very reactive. The whole thing in a perfect world... AskElephant should be intelligent where it says, if I am a VP of revenue and we're 80% of the month and I'm at 40% my number, what the heck would I want to know? Great. If I knew that, what would I want to know next? Great. What would I want to do about it? And it should go and be smart enough to think through the next five things and present that to me."
> — Rob Henderson [0:18:33]

> "Versus here's all the information, you go bento box it yourself."
> — Rob Henderson [0:19:20]

### 2. Proactive/Reactive Mode Switching (Critical — New Framework)

Rob articulated the most elegant framing of the AI-human interaction model we've heard:

> **"When I'm proactive, AI is reactive. When AI is proactive, I'm reactive."**
> — Rob Henderson [0:21:35]

This maps directly to two distinct modes:

- **User-Led (Planning Mode):** User is proactive, sets goals, asks questions. AI is reactive — fetches data, runs scenarios, presents options.
- **AI-Led (Execution Mode):** AI is proactive — surfaces issues, suggests actions, drafts responses. User is reactive — approves, adjusts, learns.

### 3. Dynamic Time-Based Experience (High)

The homepage should change based on when the user logs in and what's happening:

> "If I log in at 8AM, my home page is actually different than if I log in at 5PM."
> — Rob Henderson [0:31:28]

> "My chief of staff said, it's end of the day. You just had eight calls. Let's wrap through these 30 things that you need to get done. I already did 20 of them for you. I just need your approval on 10. Now AI is actually working with me. Holy crap. That feels so good."
> — Rob Henderson [0:31:29]

### 4. System That Learns How You Work (High)

Rob wants AskElephant to develop into a personalized system over time:

> "On the first of every month, I want time dedicated on my calendar just to sit for an hour and work together with you on all the things we should be doing this month. And that's my system."
> — Rob Henderson [0:26:57]

> "I'm training my chief of staff specifically to how I work, and it's being proactive with me to say, hey... most of the time, when people have a meeting on their calendar, if it's within five to ten minutes when you first log in, I'm actually just gonna show you what you need to be prepared for."
> — Rob Henderson [0:33:47]

> "My chief of staff knows when I need things, and it can now say, hey, this is now when you should use this new feature of AskElephant."
> — Rob Henderson [0:37:42]

### 5. Goal-to-Action Drill-Down (High)

When looking at metrics like net new ARR being down, Rob wants to drill from goal → teams → specific people → specific actions:

> "It's like I go in and click into this, and then I have the different buckets that make up whatever I clicked on. So net new ARR would be, like, sales forecast or sales, partnerships, and CS. And it would show who's doing really well, who's not. And then it's like, hey. Your direct sales is struggling. So I click on that, and I can break it down into what's actually causing this, so I can go make decisions."
> — Rob Henderson [0:13:40]

### 6. Forecast Context Over Current State (High)

Rob doesn't care where he's at — he cares whether he's going to hit:

> "I really care about, like, am I going to hit? Don't care about where I'm at. I care about, like, am I going to hit?"
> — Rob Henderson [0:16:42]

> "AskElephant should be like, hey. Your net new ARR is 1.1. You actually are forecasting that you're gonna exceed by 3,000,000 though, because x y z reason, like, I actually wouldn't be concerned about this month."
> — Rob Henderson [0:15:54]

### 7. Rapid-Fire Action Mode (High)

For reps after a day of calls, the experience should be about quickly clearing a queue:

> "Each meeting is like its own card that, like, I can, like — once I'm done, I swipe it. It's done. And AskElephant together, we can work together for ten minutes. I updated your CRM. Here's the email I drafted, and I connected the deck that you promised. Can I send it? Yes. Boom. Boom. Boom. Eight meetings done. Fifteen minutes. I just saved four hours."
> — Rob Henderson [0:25:59]

### 8. Show Value We Already Deliver (Lowest Hanging Fruit per Rob)

> "Lowest hanging fruit for biggest impact across all of our users... every single user that logged in saw, hey Tyler, I just updated these four things for you yesterday in your HubSpot instance. You also mentioned you wanted to draft an email. I did that. Here's what it looks like. Can I send it or do I adjust it?"
> — Rob Henderson [0:39:47]

> "We already have workflows that are doing things. We already have AskElephant working for people. It's just not telling them anything."
> — Rob Henderson [0:40:26]

### 9. Visual Hierarchy Critique

> "UX thing, I hate. This is so hard to follow. There's no visual hierarchy."
> — Rob Henderson [0:01:00]

> "This is, like, wasted space from a visual hierarchy in my opinion."
> — Rob Henderson [0:15:17]

### 10. Contextual Feature Introduction (Medium)

New features should be introduced in the flow of work, not as announcements:

> "On the next call that's private, it says, hey. I just came — I had a new agent that we just that I can work with now because I'm your chief of staff. It's a privacy agent. Here's what it does. I just noticed you had this call. Should we just get it activated right now? Rather than, like, introducing new privacy agent, and I have no desire to even think about that right now."
> — Rob Henderson [0:37:11]
