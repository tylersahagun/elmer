## Summary

Comprehensive competitive research expanding AskElephant's competitive lens from meeting-to-CRM tools (Fathom, Gong, Momentum) to include CRM replacement platforms (Monaco, Day.ai, Attio) and agent/automation builders (Relay.app, Gumloop, StackAI, Lindy.ai). Conducted to inform the CRM auto-update roadmap and longer-horizon platform strategy.

## Key Findings

### 1. Day.ai is the highest-threat new competitor
- Positions as "Cursor for GTM teams" with Context Graph that captures "why" behind deals
- Similar transparency/trust values to AskElephant
- Approaches from CRM replacement; AskElephant approaches from CRM enhancement (different paths to similar destination)

### 2. The "trojan horse" strategy is validated by market dynamics
- All CRM replacements (Day.ai, Attio, Monaco) require risky rip-and-replace
- AskElephant's additive approach (enhance existing CRM → accumulate signals → become source of truth) is lower friction
- Conversation-derived intelligence is the unique data asset no CRM can replicate

### 3. Workflow builder UX needs modernization
- Relay.app, Gumloop, and Lindy.ai all demonstrate UX patterns AskElephant should adopt
- HITL approvals, run history, prompt-to-workflow, and templates are table stakes for workflow builders
- AskElephant differentiates with meeting-native triggers and trust-first automation

### 4. The "notetaker" perception has a clear fix
- Lead with CRM update value ("47 updates completed, 96% accuracy") not meeting recording
- Build a Value Dashboard as the default experience
- Position recording as input, not product

## Strategic Alignment

- **Strong alignment** with product vision: "Revenue outcome system, not a note-taker"
- **Strong alignment** with board deck roadmap: Q1-Q2 Standard Agents → Q3-Q4 Seamless Integration → 2027 Platform Expansion
- **Trojan horse thesis** provides a concrete strategic narrative for platform evolution

## Problems Identified

1. AskElephant's workflow builder lacks modern UX patterns (run history, explainability, templates)
2. No quantified value narrative — competitors like Lindy claim "10 hours saved/week"
3. IC reps' preferred surface unknown (HubSpot vs. Slack vs. AskElephant)
4. No structured signal ontology defined yet (prerequisite for Phase 2)

## Feature Requests / Opportunities

1. Value Dashboard widget (updates completed, hours saved, accuracy rate)
2. Run history for workflow builder
3. Slack-first HITL approval for IC reps
4. Structured signal ontology (10 base signals per deal)
5. Enhanced HubSpot App Card showing AskElephant-exclusive signals
6. Cross-deal pattern detection
7. CRM readiness audit (validated hypothesis, no competitor serves this)
